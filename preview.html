How to Apply for OCS as an Enlisted Army Soldier

By Santino Faravelli
June 2026

A complete guide for enlisted soldiers pursuing Army Officer Candidate School (OCS), including eligibility requirements, application steps, interview preparation, and commissioning.

Introduction

Officer Candidate School (OCS) provides enlisted soldiers with a path to become commissioned officers in the United States Army.

This guide explains the process step-by-step, including eligibility requirements, preparing your application packet, interview boards, and attending OCS at Fort Moore.

Eligibility Requirements

  • United States citizenship
  • Bachelor’s degree completed before commissioning
  • Meet Army height and weight standards
  • Pass Army fitness standards
  • Eligible for a Secret clearance

Application Packet

Your packet will typically include transcripts, letters of recommendation, physical fitness scores, security clearance information, and a personal statement.

Start early. Gathering paperwork often takes longer than expected.

Interview Board

The OCS board evaluates leadership potential, professionalism, confidence, and motivation for becoming an officer.
